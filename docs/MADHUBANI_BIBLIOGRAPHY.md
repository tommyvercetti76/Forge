# Madhubani / Mithila — bibliography + provenance

The academic and cultural sources behind every master citation, body-type rule, and convention used in the Forge Madhubani catalog. This is the **provenance backing** for the "FINGERPRINT — render in the visual register of:" clause in the engine prompt.

Established 2026-05-20. Maintained as scholarship + new references are added.

## 1. Master painters cited in engine prompts

These are the four artists whose names appear in the engine's master-painter register clause. Each has primary-source documentation.

### Sita Devi (1914–2005)

> **Padma Shri (1981)** · Bihar Ratna (1984) · National Award (1969) · Kohbar wall-painting + Kachni line-style

The most internationally-recognized Mithila artist of her generation. Brought the kohbar (bridal-chamber) painting tradition out of domestic walls and into the recognized fine-art canon. Her line grammar — confident double-contour outlines with the signature almond eye — is the foundation our engine cites.

**Sources:**
- [Wikipedia: Sita Devi (painter)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sita_Devi_(painter))
- [Saffronart blog: Sita Devi, A Legendary Mithila Artist](https://blog.saffronart.com/2013/02/11/sita-devi-a-legendary-mithila-artist/) (2013)
- Mishra, S. (2022). "Mithila Art: An Analysis of Various Styles and Symbolic Values of Madhubani Painting." [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/359996237_Mithila_Art_An_Analysis_of_Various_Styles_and_Symbolic_Values_of_Madhubani_Painting)

### Ganga Devi (1928–1991)

> **Padma Shri (1984)** · National Award (1984) · Kachni-style pioneer

Born in Raseedput village, Madhubani district. Pioneer of the Kachni (line) style — fine cross-hatch line work over light or absent fills. Subject of intensive academic study by Tokio Hasegawa at the Mithila Museum Japan. Her bharni-style fill rhythm and dot-and-petal aura are cited in our engine prompt.

**Sources:**
- [Inditales: Mithila's Madhubani Artist Ganga Devi & Her Paintings](https://inditales.com/madhubani-artist-ganga-devi-mithila/)
- [Wikipedia: Madhubani art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhubani_art)
- Hasegawa, T. (Mithila Museum, Japan; founded 1989). Primary archive — see §3.

### Baua Devi (b. 1942, living master)

> **Padma Shri (2017)** · Bharni-school living master

Bharni-style narrative compositions; matsya (fish) and naga (serpent) motif vocabulary. Her work on snake and fish iconography is the primary reference for our cobra and (planned) marine-life sets.

**Sources:**
- [MAP Academy: Madhubani Painting article](https://mapacademy.io/article/madhubani-painting/)
- [Wikipedia: Madhubani art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhubani_art) (Baua Devi mentioned in master lineage)

### Mahasundari Devi (1922–2013)

> **Padma Shri (2011)** · Bharni-school + Mithila-everyday-life genre

Her Mithila-daily-life subject matter (community scenes, weddings, agricultural work) anchors the cultural specificity of the tradition. We cite her vocabulary for community-scene aesthetics even though our catalog is mostly single-subject.

**Sources:**
- [MAP Academy: Madhubani Painting](https://mapacademy.io/article/madhubani-painting/)
- Multiple pieces in Wikimedia Commons attributed to her hand (in `brand/references/madhubani/_general/`)

## 2. Academic + scholarly references

The following are the academic anchors for the catalog's taxonomy of schools, body-type conventions, and symbol vocabulary.

### Primary monographs (print)

- **Vequaud, Y.** (1977). *Women Painters of Mithila*. Thames & Hudson, London.
  Foundational Western academic monograph. Introduced Mithila painting to international audiences.

- **Anand, M. R.** (1984). *Madhubani Painting*. Publications Division, Government of India.
  Authoritative early Indian-language survey by the writer / art critic Mulk Raj Anand.

- **Brown Heinz, C.** (1990s–2000s). Multiple journal articles on the Mithila painter communities, focusing on the anthropology of women artists. (Search: ResearchGate, JSTOR, academic indexes.)

### Recent academic articles

- **Mishra, S.** (2022). "Mithila Art: An Analysis of Various Styles and Symbolic Values of Madhubani Painting." [ResearchGate publication 359996237](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/359996237_Mithila_Art_An_Analysis_of_Various_Styles_and_Symbolic_Values_of_Madhubani_Painting). The taxonomy of 5 schools (Bharni / Kachni / Tantric / Godna / Gobar) is consolidated here.

- **IJSRST** (2024). "A Review on Journey of Madhubani Painting in Present Times." [Paper 10641](https://ijsrst.com/paper/10641.pdf). Contemporary survey.

### Modern academic resource pages

- [**MAP Academy** — Madhubani Painting](https://mapacademy.io/article/madhubani-painting/). The most thorough modern academic survey of the tradition. Covers all 5 schools, the masters, the symbol vocabulary, and contemporary continuation.

- [**Wikipedia: Madhubani art**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhubani_art). General-audience starting point; well-cited.

## 3. Institutional archives

### Mithila Museum, Japan

Founded 1989 by **Tokio Hasegawa** (b. 1948). Located in Tokamachi, Niigata Prefecture, Japan. Largest single archive of Mithila / Madhubani art outside India. Holdings include works by Sita Devi, Ganga Devi, Baua Devi, Mahasundari Devi, and ~30 other masters across 5,000+ pieces.

Hasegawa's academic documentation of the tradition (especially Ganga Devi's working method) is the most rigorous scholarly resource we have access to.

- Reference: [Wikipedia: Madhubani art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhubani_art) (section on international recognition)
- The museum's published catalogs are primary-source material for the catalog's master citations.

### Mithila Art Institute (Madhubani, Bihar)

- [mithilaartinstitute.org](https://mithilaartinstitute.org)
- Trains and supports living Mithila artisans.
- The catalog's `masters.json` lists this as the primary support organization for customers who buy our designs and want to also support the living tradition.

### Other institutional holdings

- **National Museum, New Delhi** — Mithila gallery
- **Crafts Museum, Delhi** — Mithila section
- **Indira Gandhi National Centre for the Arts (IGNCA)** — Mithila documentation project

## 5. Copyright + cultural-heritage framing

Madhubani painting is **Geographically Indicated** (GI status, India) cultural heritage. The living custodians are the artisans of Mithila region in Bihar, predominantly women working in family lineages.

**The catalog's posture:**

1. **Designs are derivative, not authentic.** Forge produces "Madhubani-inspired" graphics. We never market them as authentic Mithila art.
2. **Master citations are tribute, not claim.** Our engine prompts cite Sita Devi, Ganga Devi, Baua Devi, Mahasundari Devi as stylistic anchors. This is acknowledgment of influence, not authorship transfer.
3. **The reference corpus is local-only**. Images stay on the local machine; only `.attribution.json` manifests are in git history. See `brand/references/madhubani/CURATION_GUIDE.md` for the rationale.
4. **For commercial use of LoRA-trained outputs**: customers should consult counsel and re-audit the corpus for explicit commercial permissions. Some references are CC BY-SA (requires attribution + share-alike); some are CC0 (public domain); some are GODL-India.
5. **Support the living tradition.** Every commercial use of these designs is encouraged to also support [Mithila Art Institute](https://mithilaartinstitute.org) or similar organizations.

## 7. Maintenance

This bibliography is updated when:
- A new master is added to the engine's citation block (must add primary-source link here first)
- A new reference is added to the corpus (already captured in attribution.json; rolled up here as the corpus grows)
- A new academic source supersedes / updates an earlier finding

When adding a new citation: prefer **primary sources** (museum catalogs, academic monographs) over secondary (blog posts, encyclopedia entries), and always link the canonical URL.
