# Body type: Lean quadruped

**Inherits from:** (cross-cutting; primarily mammalian Cetartiodactyla)
**Inherited by species:** sambar-deer · barasingha · chital · nilgiri-tahr · chinkara · blackbuck · bharal · nilgai

## 1. Motor pattern (the lean-quadruped silhouette)

Distinguished from heavy-quadruped (rhino-like mass) and lean-predator (cat-like agile build):

- **Body shape:** Slender, elongated, prey-animal build. Narrow chest relative to body length. Sloped or level back depending on species.
- **Limb proportions:** **SLENDER LEGS** — long, thin, designed for sustained running. Visible joints (knee, hock) clearly articulated. Heavy-quadruped pillar-legs are wrong here.
- **Neck:** Long and slender, often held vertical at rest.
- **Hooves:** **CLOVEN HOOVES** (2 main toes + 2 dewclaws) for all cervidae and bovidae. Render as folk-painted half-moon pair at bottom of each leg.
- **Coat:** Variable — usually short hair, often patterned (chital spots, sambar uniform brown, blackbuck dichromatic).
- **Pelage:** Sleek (not shaggy). Tail short to moderate, often with light underside (alarm flag).
- **Locomotion:** Unguligrade (walks on tip of hoof). Designed for high-speed bounding when alarmed.

## 2. Anatomical count constraints (limb-missing fix)

For ALL species inheriting lean-quadruped in **side profile standing-alert**:

## 3. Decoration grammar (Mithila register for lean quadrupeds)

- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones). Lean quadrupeds have moderate body surface; full ornate density risks visually overwhelming the slender silhouette.
- **Required zones (priority order):**
  1. Tikka medallion on forehead (below antler base if antlered)
  2. Dot-band collar at neck
  3. Saddle/back lotus panel
  4. Joint anklets at every knee + hock
  5. *Species-specific:* coat-pattern preservation (chital spots, blackbuck dichromatism) overlaid on indigo
- **Body BETWEEN zones:** Clean saturated indigo field, with species pattern preserved where canonical.

## 4. Default pose preferences (cited)

Lean-quadruped poses:
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — head up, ears forward, all 4 legs visible
- **side-profile-grazing** — head down, body relaxed (cervids, bovids)
- **side-profile-trotting** — one foreleg + opposite hindleg slightly raised
- **chinkara-leaping** — pronking, all four legs off ground (gazelle signature; species-specific only)
- **lying-with-head-up** — resting, legs folded under body, head alert (deer signature rest pose)

**AVOID:**
- Rearing (bovidae and cervidae rarely rear; rendered as wrong species behavior)
- Sitting on haunches (lean quadrupeds rest lying, NOT sitting)
- Stalking (these are prey, not predator; never)
