# Body type: Heavy quadruped

**Inherits from:** (cross-cutting; primarily mammalian)
**Inherited by species:** elephant · rhino · wild-water-buffalo · gaur · sundarbans-tiger (taxonomically lean but ecologically heavy by mass — but uses lean-predator body) · saltwater-crocodile (separate body type really; listed here for cross-reference)

## 1. Motor pattern (the heavy-quadruped silhouette)

Distinguished from lean-predator and lean-quadruped by **mass and pillar-like leg structure**:

- **Body shape:** Massive, thick-set, often barrel-bodied. Wide chest, broad hips.
- **Limb proportions:** **PILLAR LEGS** — broad at shoulder + hip, tapering only moderately to a wide foot. NEVER stick-thin like deer legs. NEVER long lean like cat legs. The thickness of the leg should visibly support the body mass suggested.
- **Joint visibility:** Less prominent than lean-predator; knees + hocks are buried in muscle/skin mass.
- **Feet:** Broad, wide — elephant has rounded pad-feet with toenails visible; rhino has 3-toed broad feet; bovids have cloven hooves but the foot footprint is wide.
- **Pelage:** Variable — gaur and buffalo have dense short hair; rhino has near-bare thick skin (folk-paint as flat indigo); elephant has near-bare thick skin with sparse hair.
- **Locomotion:** Plantigrade (elephant), unguligrade (rhino, bovids). All ground-bound, slow but powerful.
- **Tail:** Present, but generally short relative to body — rhino has thin tail with terminal tuft; elephant has tail with terminal hair tuft; buffalo/gaur have moderate tail with tuft.

## 2. Anatomical count constraints (limb-missing fix)

For ALL species inheriting heavy-quadruped in **side profile standing-alert**:

| Feature | Required count | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Legs visible | 4 | All 4 pillar legs MUST be visible. With heavy quadruped's wide stance, overlap is less common than in lean predators — the 4 silhouettes should be more distinct. |
| Eyes | 1 visible (side profile) | Lateral eye, almond folk-shape |
| Ears | 2 visible | Both visible from side (one in front, one behind) — large ears are common in this body type (elephant, buffalo) |
| Tail | 1 | Short to moderate, visible at rump |
| Mouth | 1, closed | Tusks visible for elephant (males); no fangs / dental hallucination |

## 3. Decoration grammar (Mithila register for heavy quadrupeds)

- **Decoration density:** **ornate** (6-7 zones). Heavy quadrupeds have large body surface area — full Madhubani density works without obscuring species identity.
- **Required zones (priority order):**
  1. Tikka medallion on forehead
  2. Dot-band collar at neck
  3. Large ornamental panel on back (saddle blanket — full lotus + sun + dot-border)
  4. Vine motif at shoulder
  5. Vermillion floral medallion at hip
  6. Rhythmic stripe anklets at every joint
  7. Chest panel / belly band
- **Body BETWEEN zones:** Remains clean saturated indigo color field (not all-over pattern). Even with 7 zones, the body's silhouette must read as an animal, not a textile.

## 4. Default pose preferences (cited)

Heavy-quadruped poses:
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — head up, all 4 pillar legs visible
- **side-profile-walking** — slow locomotion; one foreleg + opposite hindleg slightly raised
- **side-profile-grazing** (for bovids, elephant) — head lowered
- **side-profile-mud-bathing** (rhino, buffalo, elephant) — body partially low; advanced pose, less common in folk-art renders

**AVOID:**
- Rearing (extremely uncommon in heavy quadrupeds; bad for anatomy)
- Sitting on haunches (heavy quadrupeds don't rest like cats)
- Mid-leap (impossible for these animals)
