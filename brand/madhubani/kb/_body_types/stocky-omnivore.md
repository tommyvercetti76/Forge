# Body type: Stocky omnivore

**Inherits from:** (cross-cutting; multiple mammalian families)
**Inherited by species:** sloth-bear (Ursidae) · indian-wild-boar (Suidae) · pygmy-hog (Suidae) · indian-pangolin (Manidae — taxonomically distinct but body-form compatible)

## 1. Motor pattern (the stocky-omnivore silhouette)

A robust, mid-size body plan distinct from lean predator (cat-like) and heavy quadruped (rhino-like):

- **Body shape:** Robust, mid-size, often barrel-bodied. Wide chest, deep belly. Not slender like a cat; not pillar-legged like rhino.
- **Limb proportions:** **Short-to-moderate legs relative to body length.** Visible joints. Strong muscle mass especially in shoulders + haunches.
- **Feet:** Plantigrade (sloth-bear, ursids) — flat-foot with visible heel; OR digitigrade-low (boar, suids). Pangolin has unique digging claws.
- **Pelage:** Often shaggy or coarse (sloth-bear long shaggy black fur; boar bristly mane along spine). Pangolin has scales (unique exception — see family file).
- **Snout:** Often prominent + mobile — sloth-bear elongated lip; boar protruding snout for rooting; pangolin pointed.
- **Tail:** Variable — ursids have short stub tail; suids have tufted tail; pangolin has long scaly tail (longer than body).
- **Eyes:** Small relative to body, often somewhat sunken.
- **Ears:** Variable — sloth-bear has small rounded ears; boar has triangular alert ears.

## 2. Anatomical count constraints (limb-missing fix)

For ALL species inheriting stocky-omnivore in **side profile standing-alert**:

| Feature | Required count | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Legs visible | 4 | All 4 legs visible. With short legs + dense fur, occluded foreleg/hindleg pairs is the common failure mode. Both pairs must show. |
| Eyes | 1 visible (side profile) | Small almond folk-eye. |
| Ears | 2 visible | Both visible (small + rounded for ursids; pointed + alert for boar). |

## 3. Decoration grammar (Mithila register for stocky omnivores)

- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones). Stocky body suits moderate decoration; over-ornate obscures the species' shaggy or bristly texture.
- **Required zones (priority order):**
  1. Tikka medallion on forehead
  2. Dot-band collar on neck
  3. Saddle/back lotus
  4. Joint anklets at knees + ankles
  5. *Species-specific:* tusk emphasis for boar, chest blaze preservation for sloth-bear, scale pattern preservation for pangolin
- **Texture rendering:** Shaggy fur (sloth-bear) translated as rhythmic folk-fur-strokes (parallel curved lines); bristly back (boar) as upright spike-line pattern.

## 4. Default pose preferences (cited)

Stocky-omnivore poses:
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — head up, all 4 short legs visible
- **side-profile-walking** — moderate locomotion
- **side-profile-foraging** — head down, body engaged in feeding (boar rooting, sloth-bear nosing termite mound)
- **rearing-bipedal** — **sloth-bear signature** when threatened or feeding from height (citation: Bargali et al. 2012)
- **curled-defensive** — pangolin's signature defensive ball pose (citation: Heath 1992)

**AVOID:**
- Stalking (omnivores aren't predators; never)
- Lean-stretching pose (body is robust, not elongated)
- Sitting like a cat on haunches (these animals don't sit that way)
