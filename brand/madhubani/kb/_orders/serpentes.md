# Order: Serpentes (snakes)

**Inherits from:** (root tier, sub-order of Squamata)
**Inherited by families:** elapidae (cobras, kraits, mambas), viperidae (vipers — future), pythonidae (pythons — future)
**Includes from our 100-species catalog:** cobra (future: king-cobra, krait, russell's viper)

## 1. Anatomical ground truth (order-universal rules)

- **Limbs:** **0** — NEVER any legs, feet, claws. The single most important anatomy rule for snakes. Most common AI failure: stub legs or lizard-like appendages.
- **Body:** Elongated, cylindrical, no clear neck transition (except in cobras when hood spread — the spread hood briefly creates a flat-disc neck shape).
- **Eyes:** 2, on opposite sides of head, **no eyelids** (transparent brille / spectacle covering instead). Eyes always open in render. Pupil shape varies: round in cobras + most active diurnal snakes; vertical-slit in vipers + pythons + some nocturnal species. **Cobra = round pupil**, NOT slit.
- **Tongue:** **EXACTLY ONE forked tongue.** This is the species-defining-fail mode (especially for cobras). The tongue is a single Y-shape, forked at the tip — NEVER two parallel tongues, NEVER duplicated. Tongue is flicked out during scent-sampling; default render shows tongue extended (action shot) OR mouth closed (resting). Both are valid.
- **Teeth:** Present but mouth typically closed in renders. In striking pose, fangs may be visible in elapids/viperids — render carefully.
- **Scales:** Continuous covering, arranged in regular dorsal + ventral rows (ventral scales are large + transverse, used for locomotion). **Critically: scales are NOT individual painted dots — they're a continuous structured surface.**
- **Tail:** Tapers to a point, indistinguishable from body except by anal vent location. No fluke, no tuft, no separate tail-as-appendage.
- **Spine:** Single, no shoulder girdle, no pelvic girdle.

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (order-level)

**Minimal** to **none** in most snakes — males and females usually visually identical (slight size difference, females sometimes longer). Cobras are monomorphic. Render without sex specification.

## 3. Photo references

Maintained at species level. For snakes, 8 photos typically all show variety of poses (coiled, spread-hood, glide) rather than sex-split since dimorphism is minimal.

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Order-level pose vocabulary:
- **coiled-S** — body in S-curve or loose coil, head raised, resting/defensive
- **coiled-striking** — body coiled with head + neck raised in strike-ready position (extreme defensive)
- **spread-hooded** — **elapid-specific (cobras)**: body coiled, neck flattened into broad disc, head facing viewer or 3/4 angle
- **glide-straight** — body in flowing curves, head leading, locomotion pose

**AVOID:**
- Standing on body (snakes can't stand)
- Coiled in unnatural geometric pattern (perfect circle, perfect square)
- Any limb suggestion whatsoever

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, order-level)

- **Body fill color:** indigo `#1a2952` override (Madhubani convention)
- **Scale pattern:** Translate dorsal scale rows into **regular folk-scale pattern** — overlapping leaf-vein or diamond shapes following the body's curve. NOT individual dots. Bands of color (alternating dark + light) match natural species markings where canonical.
- **Eye character:** Almond folk-eye (snakes have round natural pupil but folk-icon convention reshapes to almond). Round pupil for cobra; vertical-slit for vipers (when added).
- **Decoration density:** **minimal** to **balanced** (3-4 zones) — over-decoration obscures the snake's serpentine flow and species markings.
- **Required zones for snakes (order-level minimum):**
  1. Head crown (small tikka or hood-marking medallion above eyes)
  2. Body scale pattern (continuous from neck to tail, folk-translated)
  3. Belly/ventral folk band (visible where body curves, lighter than dorsal)
  4. *Cobra-specific:* hood spectacle marking (V or O shape on back of hood)
