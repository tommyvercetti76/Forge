# Order: Primates

**Inherits from:** (root tier)
**Inherited by families:** cercopithecidae (Old World monkeys — macaque, golden-langur, nilgiri-langur), hylobatidae (lesser apes — hoolock-gibbon)
**Includes from our 100-species catalog:** macaque, golden-langur, nilgiri-langur, hoolock-gibbon

## 1. Anatomical ground truth (order-universal rules)

- **Limbs:** 4 (mammalian). All primates retain forelimbs and hindlimbs. **Opposable thumbs** on hands (some species have reduced thumbs — colobines).
- **Eyes:** 2, **forward-facing** (binocular vision, distinctive of primates among mammals). Almond folk-eye sits in the FRONT of the face, not on the sides like ungulates.
- **Ears:** 2, varied shapes — round (macaque), small flat (langur), large (gibbon, where mostly hidden by fur).
- **Tail:** Family-dependent. **Cercopithecidae have tails (often long)**; **hylobatidae (apes including gibbons) have NO tail** — gibbon-rendering must omit tail. Common AI hallucination: adding tail to apes.
- **Hands + feet:** 5 digits each, opposable thumb on hand, often opposable hallux on foot (cercopithecidae). Distinctive — render with visible hand-shape, not paw.
- **Posture:** Most primates **sit upright** by default (not lying or standing-quadrupedal). Quadrupedal locomotion uses palm + sole; bipedal walking is rare but possible.
- **Sexual dimorphism:** Highly variable. Macaques moderate; langurs minimal; gibbons minimal.
- **Fur:** Continuous body covering, variable colors. Face often partially bare.

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (order-level)

| Family | Dimorphism | Visual cue |
|---|---|---|
| Cercopithecidae (macaque, langur) | Subtle — moderate | Males slightly larger; some species (macaque) have visible canines. Color usually similar. |
| Hylobatidae (gibbon) | **Notable** | Hoolock gibbons: male is jet-black, **female is buff-tan** — extreme sexual dichromatism. |

**Hoolock gibbon especially**: must render male (black) and female (buff-tan) distinctly. The dichromatism is the species' identity marker.

## 3. Photo references

Maintained at species level. For hoolock gibbon, MUST include 4 male (black) + 4 female (buff-tan) photos to capture the dichromatism. For macaque, langur, 8 mixed photos with sex-balance where available.

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Order-level pose vocabulary:
- **sitting-upright** (default for most) — back straight, hands resting on legs or holding food, tail visible if present
- **branch-perch** — sitting/standing on a branch, arboreal context
- **hanging-arm-suspension** — **hylobatidae-signature**: hanging by one or both arms below a branch. Gibbon-iconic pose.
- **knuckle-walking-quadrupedal** — palms on ground, knuckles for support — not typical of cercopithecines but possible
- **bipedal-standing** — rare but possible; macaques sometimes stand upright

**AVOID:**
- Tail on gibbon (gibbons have no tail)
- Forward-leaning quadrupedal stance for macaque/langur — they sit, they don't stand quadruped-low

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, order-level)

- **Body fill color:** indigo `#1a2952` override default. **Exception for hoolock gibbon:** preserve black-vs-buff dichromatism (male black body fill, female buff-tan body fill) — species identity overrides convention.
- **Face rendering:** Bare-face primates (macaque) — render face in a lighter folk-skin tone with prominent expressive features (eyes, brow, mouth shape). Folk-eye is still almond + watchful.
- **Hand rendering:** Visible 5-finger hands, opposable thumb visible. NEVER paw. NEVER hoof.
- **Tail rendering:** When present (cercopithecidae), tail is long and often held in characteristic species-specific position. NEVER added to gibbons.
- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones)
- **Required zones (order-level for primates):**
  1. Tikka medallion on forehead/crown
  2. Body color field with strategic dot accents
  3. Shoulder vine motif
  4. Tail-band rhythm (where tail present)
  5. *Family-specific decoration* — see family files
