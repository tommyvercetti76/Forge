# Order: Cetacea (whales, dolphins, porpoises)

**Inherits from:** (root tier — placed within Cetartiodactyla)
**Inherited by families:** delphinidae (dolphins), and future families (mysticeti for baleen whales)
**Includes from our 100-species catalog:** irrawaddy-dolphin · whale-shark *(taxonomic note: whale-shark is NOT Cetacea — it's a cartilaginous fish in Rhincodontidae. See its own family file. Listed here only for cross-reference.)*

## 1. Anatomical ground truth (order-universal rules)

- **Limbs:** **NO hindlimbs (vestigial only, invisible).** Forelimbs are modified into **pectoral fins (flippers)** — flat, paddle-shaped. So in side profile, the body has: 1 visible pectoral fin (side), 1 dorsal fin (top), and a fluke (horizontal tail). NEVER any legs or visible four-limb structure.
- **Body:** Fully streamlined, fusiform (torpedo-shaped). Smooth surface, no fur, no scales.
- **Eyes:** 2, on lateral sides of head. Small relative to body.
- **Blowhole:** 1 (or 2 in baleen whales) on top of head — render visible from above or 3/4 angle, not in pure side profile.
- **Beak / rostrum:** Family-specific — dolphins have prominent forward-pointing rostrum (delphinids); irrawaddy-dolphin is unusual — rounded blunt head (NO prominent rostrum).
- **Tail / fluke:** Horizontal tail flukes (NOT vertical like fish). The fluke is at the END of the body, oriented like wings perpendicular to the body's vertical axis.
- **Dorsal fin:** Family-specific shape and size. Irrawaddy-dolphin has small triangular dorsal fin set far back.
- **Teeth:** Present in toothed whales (Odontoceti) including dolphins. Mouth typically rendered closed.

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (order-level)

**Minimal** in most cetaceans. Males slightly larger but visually similar. Render without sex specification. Photo references for cetaceans typically don't sex-tag.

## 3. Photo references

Maintained at species level — 8 photos showing pose variety (surfacing, swimming-side, breaching where applicable) rather than sex split.

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Order-level pose vocabulary:
- **swimming-side** (default) — body horizontal, profile view, pectoral fin + dorsal fin + fluke all visible
- **surfacing** — body partially above water, blowhole + dorsal fin visible, water context implied
- **breaching** — body partially or fully out of water, leaping vertical — common for large cetaceans, less for irrawaddy
- **resting-floating** — body horizontal at surface, calm pose

**AVOID:**
- Vertical-tail-fish-style rendering (cetacean fluke is HORIZONTAL, fish tail is VERTICAL)
- Limbs of any kind
- Standing posture

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, order-level)

- **Body fill color:** indigo `#1a2952` override default. Natural cetacean color is typically grey — indigo override defeats the model's grey-marine-mammal prior.
- **Water context:** Madhubani folk tradition has strong fish + water iconography. Cetacean renders can include folk-water elements (wave lines, fish companions) as background — culturally appropriate.
- **Fluke emphasis:** The horizontal fluke is the cetacean's signature anatomical feature; render with visible flat horizontal plane (folk-flat-color panel).
- **Eye character:** Almond folk-eye, small relative to body. Watchful but not aggressive.
- **Decoration density:** **minimal** to **balanced** (3-4 zones). Cetacean bodies are smooth-skinned without natural patterns — over-decoration is unnatural for the form.
- **Required zones for cetaceans (order-level minimum):**
  1. Head/blowhole-area tikka medallion
  2. Body color field with subtle folk-pattern flow lines (suggesting motion)
  3. Dorsal-fin folk band
  4. Fluke decorative pattern (folk-stripes on the horizontal fluke surface)
