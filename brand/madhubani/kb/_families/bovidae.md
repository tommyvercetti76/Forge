# Family: Bovidae (cattle, antelope, sheep, goats)

**Inherits from:** (mammalia, within Cetartiodactyla)
**Inherited by species:** gaur, nilgai, wild-water-buffalo, bharal, chinkara, blackbuck, nilgiri-tahr (and future urial)
**Operationally inherits body types:** lean-quadruped (chinkara, blackbuck, nilgai) OR heavy-quadruped (gaur, buffalo) OR a special "stocky-quadruped" tier for the goats (bharal, nilgiri-tahr)

## 1. Family-universal anatomical features

The key feature that distinguishes Bovidae from Cervidae: **horns are PERMANENT, KERATINOUS, UNBRANCHED** (mostly).

- **Horns:** Bony core + keratin sheath. **Never shed** (vs cervid antlers which shed annually). **Both sexes have horns in most bovids**, though males' are typically larger. Architecture varies:
  - **gaur:** Heavy massive curved horns with bony boss (skull-fused base)
  - **wild-water-buffalo:** Wide crescent-curve horns sweeping outward
  - **nilgai:** Small curved horns, **MALES ONLY** (females are hornless) — rare exception for Bovidae
  - **bharal (blue sheep):** Outward-then-downward-curved horns
  - **chinkara:** Slender ringed horns, lyre-shaped curves
  - **blackbuck:** **Spiral twisted horns**, can reach 70 cm — the species' iconic feature
  - **nilgiri-tahr:** Stout backward-curved horns
- **Hooves:** Cloven (split) — same as cervids (2 main toes + 2 dewclaws).
- **Body proportions:** Variable by sub-family:
  - **Heavy bovids (gaur, buffalo):** Massive, muscular, short-legged relative to body mass
  - **Antelope bovids (nilgai, chinkara, blackbuck):** Lean, long-legged, swift
  - **Caprines / goats (bharal, nilgiri-tahr):** Compact, stocky, mountain-adapted
- **Eyes:** 2, laterally positioned. Almond shape with visible white margin (prey animal). Pupil is **horizontal rectangular slit** (distinctive of bovids — render as horizontal almond pupil inside outer almond eye).
- **Ears:** 2, "deer-like" but variable. Larger and more mobile in antelope-type bovids.
- **Tail:** Variable — short tuft in cattle types, longer tassel in antelope types.
- **Coat colors:** Species-diagnostic; see species files. Key visual markers:
  - blackbuck male: black above + white below (sexual dichromatism)
  - nilgai male: blue-grey (literal "blue bull")
  - gaur: dark coffee-black body + white socks (lower legs)

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (family-level)

Highly variable. Strong dimorphism in some bovids:

| Species | Dimorphism | Visual cue |
|---|---|---|
| blackbuck | **Extreme** | Male: black-and-white spiral-horned. Female: tawny with no/small horns. |
| nilgai | **Strong** | Male: blue-grey body + horns. Female: tawny body + hornless. |
| gaur | Subtle | Males larger + darker; both sexes horned. |
| buffalo | Subtle | Males slightly larger; both sexes horned (females sometimes larger horns). |
| bharal, chinkara, nilgiri-tahr | Moderate | Males larger + heavier horns; both sexes horned. |

For **blackbuck and nilgai especially**: MUST render male and female distinctly. Default render is male (visually iconic).

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Bovidae pose vocabulary:
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — universal, all species
- **side-profile-grazing** — head down, body relaxed (cattle and antelope types)
- **side-profile-rutting** — male threat display; varies by species
- **chinkara-leaping** — Indian gazelle's signature pronking leap; valid only for chinkara
- **bharal-mountain-perch** — mountain goat poses on rocky terrain

**AVOID:**
- Rearing (most bovids don't rear; goats can but it's uncommon)
- Stalking (bovids are NOT predators; never)

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, bovidae-specific)

- **Body fill color:** indigo `#1a2952` override default. **Exceptions where natural color is species identity:**
  - blackbuck male: black + white dichromatism preserved (this IS the species identity)
  - nilgai male: blue-grey preserved (literal "blue bull"; defeats the override)
  - gaur: dark coffee-black + WHITE STOCKINGS preserved
- **Horn rendering:** Folk-painted with species-specific architecture:
  - Spiral horns (blackbuck): rendered as multiple concentric curves stacking upward
  - Boss horns (gaur): rendered as massive curved arcs joined at base
  - Crescent horns (buffalo): rendered as wide outward sweeps
- **Hoof rendering:** Cloven hoof as 2 folk-painted half-moons.
- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones) for antelopes; **ornate** (6-7 zones) for the heavy bovids (gaur, buffalo) — their large body allows more decoration.
- **Required zones (varies by sub-type):**
  - Antelope bovids: tikka + collar + saddle + anklets
  - Heavy bovids: + chest panel + flank vine + hip floral (richer decoration)
