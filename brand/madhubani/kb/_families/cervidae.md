# Family: Cervidae (true deer)

**Inherits from:** (mammalia, no order file currently — cervidae sits within Cetartiodactyla which is shared with bovidae and cetacea; we model each at family tier for clarity)
**Inherited by species:** sambar-deer, barasingha, chital
**Operationally inherits body type:** [_body_types/lean-quadruped.md](../_body_types/lean-quadruped.md)

## 1. Family-universal anatomical features

- **Antlers (MALES ONLY):** Bony, shed annually. Branched architecture. **The single most important Cervidae feature.** Number of points varies by species + age:
  - chital (3-tined): 3 points per side typically (brow + back + crown)
  - sambar (3-tined heavy): 3 points per side, very robust
  - barasingha (multi-tined): 10-15 points total — "twelve-tined" is the name's literal meaning ("barasingha" = "12-horned")
- **Antlerless (FEMALES):** Render with **clean smooth heads** — no horn stubs, no fake antlers. Females must be visually identifiable as antlerless deer.
- **Hooves:** Cloven (split) — 2 main toes + 2 dewclaws higher on leg. NEVER a single hoof (that's horse). NEVER paws (that's predator).
- **Body proportions:** Lean-quadruped with **slender legs** (vs heavy-quadruped's pillar legs). Long neck. Narrow body relative to height.
- **Eyes:** 2, **laterally positioned** (prey animal — wide field of view). Almond shape natural. Visible eye-white margins (different from carnivore eye character).
- **Ears:** 2, large, mobile, "deer-shape" — long, pointed, often rendered alert.
- **Tail:** Short to moderate (chital ~20cm, sambar ~25cm). Often pale underside (white "flag" when alarmed).
- **Coat patterns:** Species-variable:
  - chital: rust-red with **white spots** (the spots persist into adulthood — diagnostic)
  - sambar: uniform dark brown, NO spots
  - barasingha: golden-brown, NO spots
- **Mouth:** Closed by default; no tusks (despite "musk deer" which we don't have).

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (family-level)

**Strong** — antlers in males only is the key dimorphic feature.

| Species | Male | Female |
|---|---|---|
| chital | Multi-tined antlers, slightly larger body | Antlerless, slightly smaller |
| sambar | Heavy 3-tined antlers, shaggy neck mane | Antlerless, less mane |
| barasingha | 10-15-tined antlers (extreme!) | Antlerless |

**Render decision:** Default to **male** for primary species image (antlers are visually iconic for cervids). Include female photos in the 8-photo set to capture the antlerless variant.

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Cervidae pose vocabulary (refines lean-quadruped):
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — head up, ears forward, all 4 legs visible. Most common rendering.
- **side-profile-grazing** — head down, body relaxed. Common natural pose.
- **side-profile-trotting** — moderate locomotion; one foreleg + opposite hindleg raised in mid-step
- **rutting-male-display** — antlers raised, head back, neck arched (only valid for males in mating season; advanced pose)

**AVOID:**
- Rearing (deer don't rear except in extreme threat)
- Sitting upright (deer rest lying down, not sitting on haunches)

Citation: Putman (1988); Geist (1998) for pose vocabulary; Mattioli (2011) for species-specific behaviors.

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, cervidae-specific)

- **Body fill color:** indigo `#1a2952` override default. **Exception:** chital's white spots — preserve the spot pattern overlaid on indigo as white folk-dots.
- **Antler rendering:** Render as **folk-painted branched lines** — clean linework, not naturalistic bone texture. Each tine is a clear branch of the antler.
- **Hoof rendering:** Cloven hoof rendered as 2 folk-painted half-moons at the bottom of each leg.
- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones) — preserves species pattern (chital spots) while adding ornament.
- **Required zones for cervidae:**
  1. tikka medallion on forehead (above eyes, below antlers)
  2. dot-band collar on neck
  3. saddle/back lotus
  4. anklets at every joint (knee, hock)
  5. *chital-specific:* white-spot pattern preserved overlay
