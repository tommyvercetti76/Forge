# Family: Phasianidae (pheasants, peafowl, junglefowl, partridges)

**Inherits from:** [_orders/aves.md](../_orders/aves.md)
**Inherited by species:** peacock (Indian peafowl, *Pavo cristatus*); future: red junglefowl, cheer pheasant, kalij pheasant, monal
**Body type:** bird (terrestrial-ground)

## 1. Family-universal anatomical features (deltas from Aves)

Phasianidae are **terrestrial galliform birds** — distinct from most Aves in that they're primarily ground-dwellers, with weak short-range flight.

- **Legs:** Strong, **scaled, often spurred (males)**. Visible drumstick, dark scales below the feather line. Spurs are bony projections on the back of male legs (used in fighting) — **male-only feature**.
- **Wings:** Short, rounded, designed for explosive short flight. Held tight against body when on ground.
- **Tail:** Highly variable by species and sex:
  - **Peacock (male):** **Train of elongated tail-covert feathers, NOT actual tail**, can reach 1.5 m. Includes 100-150 "eye-spot" (ocellus) ornaments. **THE iconic feature.**
  - **Peahen (female):** Short, plain tail — no train.
  - **Pheasants:** Long graduated tail, often striking pattern.
  - **Partridges:** Short stout tail.
- **Crown:** Many phasianids have **head crests / crowns**. Peacock has the iconic spray-crest. Pheasants vary widely.
- **Beak:** Strong, slightly curved downward at tip. Designed for ground-feeding (grain, insects).
- **Feet:** Strong, 3 forward toes + 1 back toe (anisodactyl, standard). Scratching feet — used to scrape ground for food.
- **Coloration:** **Strong sexual dichromatism** is the family hallmark (see below).

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (family-level)

**Extreme** in most Phasianidae. The male is the visually iconic form (full plumage display); the female is cryptic / drab.

| Species | Male | Female |
|---|---|---|
| Peacock | Full iridescent train (1.5m), iridescent blue neck, blue body, iridescent throat, head crest | Brown/grey body, short tail, no train, simple crest |
| Cheer pheasant | Multicolored barring, long tail | Brown cryptic plumage |
| Red junglefowl | Red/golden plumage, dark green tail, comb + wattles | Brown cryptic |

**Default render for peacock = MALE** (the male IS the species' folk-art identity). Render female if explicitly asked, but 4 of 8 photos should still capture female form for completeness.

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Phasianidae pose vocabulary:
- **standing-side-profile** (default) — male shows compressed train hanging behind
- **displaying-full-fan** — **PEACOCK SIGNATURE**: train spread wide in courtship display. The visual icon of the species + the family. (Citations: Petrie 1994; Loyau et al. 2008.)
- **walking-side** — ground locomotion, both legs visible
- **perching-low** — on a low branch or rock, body upright
- **strutting** — display walk, neck stretched, train slightly raised

**AVOID:**
- Mid-flight (galliforms fly poorly + briefly; mid-flight pose is wrong for the family)
- Two-legged display with train (display is universally male; female has no train)

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, phasianidae-specific)

- **Body fill color:** **EXCEPTION to indigo override** for peacock. Peacock's iridescent multi-color plumage is the species identity — **preserve the full multi-color palette** (emerald + sapphire + gold + vermillion + bronze). The body fill becomes a riot of folk colors.
- **Train rendering (PEACOCK CRITICAL):** The train must be the **dominant visual element** of the composition, occupying 60%+ of frame width. **NEVER stubby, NEVER partial, NEVER missing.** The eye-spots (ocelli) are rendered as concentric folk-medallions in indigo + vermillion + green.
- **Crown rendering:** Peacock's spray-crest is a delicate fan of 3-5 wire-like feathers with terminal disks — render as folk linework with small medallions at each tip.
- **Eye character:** Almond folk-eye in cream + black (NOT iridescent — keep the eye character anchored even when body is multicolored).
- **Decoration density:** **ornate** (6-7 zones) — the train itself IS most of the decoration, supplemented by body + crown + neck details.
- **Required zones for phasianidae:**
  1. Crown crest (head fan of disks)
  2. Iridescent neck panel
  3. Body color riot
  4. **Train fan (peacock-CRITICAL)** with eye-spot pattern
  5. Wing-shoulder folk-feather panel
  6. Leg/foot folk-line detail
  7. Tail-base junction medallion
