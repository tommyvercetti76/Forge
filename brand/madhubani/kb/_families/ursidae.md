# Family: Ursidae (bears)

**Inherits from:** [_orders/carnivora.md](../_orders/carnivora.md)
**Inherited by species:** sloth-bear (*Melursus ursinus*); future: Himalayan brown bear, Asiatic black bear
**Operationally inherits body type:** [_body_types/stocky-omnivore.md](../_body_types/stocky-omnivore.md)

## 1. Family-universal anatomical features (deltas from Carnivora)

Ursids are **large omnivorous carnivorans** distinct from cats and dogs in body plan:

- **Body shape:** Stocky, robust, deep chest, often broad shoulders. NOT lean and elongated like felids/canids.
- **Limbs:** 4, **plantigrade** (walks on flat soles, like humans — feet visibly flat on ground). Distinguishes ursids from digitigrade felids/canids in side profile.
- **Feet:** Broad with visible footpad + 5 toes per foot. **Long curved claws** (non-retractile, unlike felids) — used for digging, climbing, food extraction. Sloth-bear has the longest claws in the family relative to body size (adapted for termite excavation).
- **Snout:** Elongated relative to felids. Sloth-bear has extreme mobile lip/snout — adapted for sucking termites.
- **Ears:** Small, rounded — NEVER pointed-tall like canids or triangular-erect like felids.
- **Tail:** **Short stub** — NEVER long. A bear with a long tail is a felid-hallucination.
- **Coat:** Dense, often shaggy. Color is family-diagnostic:
  - Sloth-bear: **shaggy black** with white V chest blaze
  - Future Himalayan brown bear: brown-grey
  - Future Asiatic black bear: black with crescent chest mark (similar to sloth-bear's V)
- **Mouth:** Closed by default; no tusks; canines moderate.

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (family-level)

**Moderate** — males ~25-40% heavier than females. **Visually similar** otherwise (color, marking, body shape). Render without sex specification by default.

## 3. Photo references

Maintained at species level — 8 photos per species, typically mixed-pose (rather than sex-split) since dimorphism is moderate-not-extreme.

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Ursidae pose vocabulary:
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — head up, all 4 plantigrade legs visible
- **side-profile-foraging** — head down, body engaged in feeding (sloth-bear at termite mound classic)
- **rearing-bipedal** — **URSID-SIGNATURE** when threatened, looking for food, or reaching height. Sloth-bear especially uses this for defensive display.
- **sitting-upright** — possible, less iconic than rearing
- **walking-slow** — moderate locomotion

**AVOID:**
- Mid-leap (bears don't leap dynamically)
- Stalking-low (bears aren't ambush predators)
- Long-tail rendering (bears have short stub tails)

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, ursid-specific)

- **Body fill color:** Indigo `#1a2952` override for default; **EXCEPTION for sloth-bear** which keeps its black fur (defends species identity against brown-bear drift).
- **Chest blaze (sloth-bear CRITICAL):** Preserve the WHITE V chest blaze as a folk-white panel; it's the species-identifying mark.
- **Claw rendering:** Long curved claws visible at the end of each foot — folk-painted as 3-5 curved lines.
- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones). Bear bodies are large; some decoration works but the shaggy fur texture itself is part of the species identity.
- **Required zones for ursids:**
  1. Tikka medallion on forehead/crown
  2. Body fur translated as rhythmic folk-fur strokes (parallel curved lines)
  3. *Sloth-bear specific:* Chest blaze preserved
  4. Joint anklets on legs
  5. Snout/lip emphasis (especially for sloth-bear)
