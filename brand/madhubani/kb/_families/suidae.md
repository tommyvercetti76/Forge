# Family: Suidae (pigs, wild boars)

**Inherits from:** (mammalia, within Cetartiodactyla)
**Inherited by species:** indian-wild-boar (*Sus scrofa cristatus*), pygmy-hog (*Porcula salvania*)
**Operationally inherits body type:** [_body_types/stocky-omnivore.md](../_body_types/stocky-omnivore.md)

## 1. Family-universal anatomical features

Suids are **omnivorous artiodactyls** (even-toed ungulates) — distinct from cetacean / bovid / cervid relatives:

- **Body shape:** Stocky, barrel-chested. Mature males have a distinctive sloped back (shoulders higher than hips).
- **Limbs:** 4, slender relative to body mass, with **cloven hooves** (2 main toes + 2 dewclaws — same artiodactyl pattern as cervidae and bovidae). NOT pillar-legs like rhino/elephant.
- **Snout:** **Elongated, mobile, terminating in a flat disc nose** — adapted for rooting in soil. THE distinguishing feature visible in side profile.
- **Tusks:** **PERMANENT enlarged canines** in mature males — **upper tusks grow backward + outward**, **lower tusks grow upward and curve out**. Both pairs typically visible from side profile in adult males. THE iconic species feature for wild boar. Pygmy-hog has SMALL tusks (subtle) — not iconic.
- **Eyes:** 2, small relative to body, lateral.
- **Ears:** 2, triangular, alert/forward in stance.
- **Tail:** Moderate length with terminal tuft (different from rhino's thin tail; different from bear's stub).
- **Coat:** Short bristly hair, often dark. **Erect dorsal bristle ridge / mane** along spine, especially in males ("cristatus" subspecies name refers to this crest in Indian boar).
- **Compact body proportions:** Body short relative to depth — wild boar are compact and powerful.

## 2. Sexual dimorphism (family-level)

**Moderate** — male wild boar develop large prominent tusks, females have only small/hidden tusks (incipient). Males are also ~20-30% heavier and develop the prominent dorsal bristle crest more strongly. **Default render: MALE (tusks are the iconic species feature).** Female photos still included in 8-photo set for completeness.

Pygmy-hog dimorphism is subtle (no large tusks in either sex).

## 3. Photo references

## 4. Pose preferences (cited)

Suidae pose vocabulary:
- **side-profile-standing-alert** (default) — sloped back visible, snout forward, tusks visible (male)
- **side-profile-foraging** — head down with snout rooting in soil (the iconic boar pose)
- **side-profile-rutting-display** — bristles raised, head up, tusks prominent (male threat display)
- **side-profile-walking** — moderate locomotion
- **family-group-rendering** — sow with piglets (when culturally appropriate)

**AVOID:**
- Stalking (boars are omnivores, not predators)
- Tail-curled-up like a domestic pig (wild boar tail is straight with tuft, not curly)
- Long-leg slender rendering (boars are compact + powerful)

## 5. Folk-art conversion (Mithila register, suid-specific)

- **Body fill color:** indigo `#1a2952` override.
- **Tusk emphasis (CRITICAL for wild boar male):** **Both prominent curved tusks** clearly visible on either side of the mouth as folk-painted curving lines.
- **Dorsal bristle ridge:** Translate the "cristatus" mane as a rhythmic folk-spike line along the back — clean upright lines, not naturalistic hair.
- **Snout emphasis:** The elongated snout is a defining feature — render with clear visible disc-nose terminating the elongated muzzle.
- **Hoof rendering:** Cloven hoof as 2 folk-half-moons (standard artiodactyl).
- **Decoration density:** **balanced** (4-5 zones); avoid covering tusks or bristle ridge.
- **Required zones for suids:**
  1. Tikka medallion on forehead
  2. Dot-band collar at neck
  3. Saddle/back lotus (centered, between bristle ridge segments)
  4. Joint anklets
  5. *Male-specific:* tusk emphasis preserved + dorsal bristle ridge
